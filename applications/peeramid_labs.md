### Overview

- **Tagline**:  Advancing Decentralized Governance: A Deep Dive into Competence Identification and Ranking Protocols with a Focus on Privacy and the Polkadot Ecosystem
- **Description**: This project aims to refine and expand upon our existing research on a novel, decentralized protocol for identifying and ranking competence within DAOs. We will delve deeper into the cryptographic foundations, explore privacy-preserving techniques using Multi-Party Computation (MPC), and analyze the specific challenges and opportunities within the Polkadot DAO ecosystem.
- **Integration with Substrate/Polkadot/Kusama**: This research will directly benefit the Polkadot and Kusama ecosystems by providing a deeper understanding of DAO governance challenges and proposing solutions tailored to Substrate-based chains. While not directly implementing a Substrate pallet in this grant, the findings will lay the groundwork for future integrations.
- **Interest**:  We are passionate about advancing decentralized governance and empowering communities through robust and transparent decision-making mechanisms. This research aligns with our commitment to building a more equitable and efficient Web3 ecosystem.
- **Research Type**: This project will result in a refined and expanded research paper, potentially leading to publications in peer-reviewed academic venues and presentations at relevant conferences.

### Project Details

**Problems to Investigate**:

- **Limitations of current DAO governance mechanisms:**  Many DAOs face challenges with low participation, centralization risks, and the need for more nuanced competence assessment.
- **Privacy concerns in competence identification:**  Balancing transparency with the need to protect sensitive information about individual competence and contributions is crucial.
- **Unique challenges and opportunities in the Polkadot ecosystem:** Understanding the specific governance landscape of Substrate-based chains and identifying areas for improvement.

**Research Questions/Hypothesis**:

- **Can we build an autonomous competence identification protocol based on the assumption of available modern technologies such as MPC, TEE, and DLTs?**  This explores the feasibility of leveraging advanced cryptographic techniques to enhance DAO governance.
- **Can there be a cryptographic protocol designed that ensures a rich user experience, as described in our initial work, and ensures privacy constraints without relying on a central actor or TEE?**  This investigates privacy-preserving approaches to competence identification and ranking.
- **What are the current levels of centralization, participation, and agenda setter diversity in the Polkadot ecosystem?** This analyzes the specific challenges and opportunities within Substrate-based DAOs.

**Methodology**:

- **Phase 1: MPC Research and Formal Verification (3 months):**
    - Conduct in-depth research on the application of MPC techniques to privacy-preserving competence identification and ranking.
    - Formally verify the security and privacy properties of the proposed MPC-based protocols.
    - Explore the feasibility and trade-offs of different MPC approaches in the context of DAO governance.
- **Phase 2:  Refinement of Existing Research and Mathematical Verification (1 month):**
    - Refine and expand our existing research paper based on new findings and community feedback.
    - Rigorously verify the mathematical foundations of the proposed competence identification and ranking mechanisms.
    - Analyze the protocol's properties, including fairness, transparency, and resistance to manipulation.
- **Phase 3: Polkadot Ecosystem Analysis (1 month):**
    - Conduct a comprehensive analysis of the Polkadot DAO ecosystem, including participation rates, governance structures, and levels of centralization.
    - Identify specific challenges and opportunities for applying our research to Substrate-based chains.
    - Compare and contrast the Polkadot ecosystem with other DAO landscapes to highlight unique considerations.

**Data Collection and Analysis Procedures**:

- Analyze existing research and literature on MPC, TEE, and DLTs in the context of decentralized governance.
- Utilize formal verification tools and techniques to analyze the security and privacy properties of the proposed protocols.
- Gather and analyze data from the Polkadot ecosystem, including on-chain governance activity, participation metrics, and DAO structures.

**Expected Results:**

- A refined and expanded research paper incorporating new findings on MPC, formal verification, and analysis of the Polkadot ecosystem.
- A deeper understanding of the challenges and opportunities for applying competence identification and ranking protocols in Substrate-based chains.
- Potential contributions to the broader Web3 community through publications, presentations, and open-source tools.
